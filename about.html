Bangladesh Crime Data Context

Crime data in Bangladesh is published yearly by Bangladesh Police and the Bangladesh Bureau of Statistics (BBS), covering national, divisional, district, and upazila levels. Key crime types include dacoity, robbery, murder, narcotics, gender-based violence, and more.

About the Crime Data Analysis Project

This project analyzes crime data in Bangladesh. The purpose is to enable users to see crime trends (male vs female victims, yearly data, district-wise crime rate) and visualize them with charts, tables, and graphs.

Methodology

Sources include Bangladesh Police official reports, BBS demographic statistics, NGO reports, and media data. Data is collected from police stations, aggregated at district/division level, and inconsistencies are handled using secondary estimations. A glossary for local crime terminology (GD, FIR, case types) will be included.

Data Caveats

Issues include delayed reporting from some police stations, underreporting, and limited public data access. Data is aggregated from 64 districts and metropolitan areas, including Dhaka and Chattogram, based on official publications by Bangladesh Police Headquarters or BBS summaries.

Supplementing Data

Missing reports from upazilas or stations are estimated using divisional summaries. Some upazilas report late, but division-level annual summaries are used to fill gaps for more complete analysis.

Non-Crime Data

Population data is sourced from BBS, and administrative boundaries (divisions, districts, upazilas) are defined according to government records.

Transparency & Contact

Code and data for this project are open-source. For questions or collaboration, contact the team at ibrahimrafi963@gmail.com.

Caution Against Ranking

Bangladesh Police and BBS discourage direct ranking of districts due to variation in reporting practices and population base. Data should be interpreted with caution and in context.
